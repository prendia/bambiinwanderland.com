About

Recently, I found myself in the midst of an ocean. After completing two degrees, still working my part-time retail job, I was left with a whole bunch of time on my hands. I had been swimming against my own tide, and for what? In this free time, I realised I'd been following a stream of water that was paved by others, my parents, partners, society, social media, the whole bloody lot.

Up until 2025, I swam that stream pretty well. But what I didn't realise was that every time I kicked my legs, I'd hook a stone to the bottom of my shoe. By the time the stream met the sea, I had so many stones attached to me that I could no longer stay afloat.

Meeting the sea was tough. All this weight in stones, no shore to swim back to. Little did I know back in my stream-swimming days, the stream only flows one way. God do I know it now.

When you're in the middle of the ocean, there's no working compass. Everything you thought you had is wet and broken. You can pick a direction and swim, hoping for land. Swim in circles, looking but never moving. Wait for magical rescue. Or sink as far as the stones will pull you.

I swam around in circles for a while. Then my body got tired. I was pulled down; pulled so far that there was no light left to see. But down there in the dark, I found these weird fish. The ones that make their own light. They come and go pretty quickly, but they exist.

So, I wonder: can I create my own light like those little fish? Perhaps yes, perhaps no. But I'm going to try.
